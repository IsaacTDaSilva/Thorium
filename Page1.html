What is Thorium

Thorium energy is a type of nuclear energy. Nuclear energy is generated by using radioactive material to convert water to steam. That steam then turns turbines which generate electricity. In this whole process, little to no carbon dioxide is produced. Since nuclear power plants use a minable, storable material, as fuel, nuclear energy is very reliable. Additionally, it is highly energy-efficient compared even to fossil fuels.

One of the largest reasons that thorium energy has not been implemented is because of fear of nuclear power. After Chernobyl and Three Mile Island, public opinion of nuclear power has become negative. While it is true that at the time nuclear power was somewhat dangerous, with modern technology nuclear energy is far safer than fossil fuels. Thorium is an especially safe fuel source, so much so that it would be virtually impossible for a thorium reactor meltdown to take place.
